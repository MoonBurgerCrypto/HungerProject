What is Starvation?

Starvation is an extreme form of malnutrition where the body lacks essential nutrients and calories to function. It results from long-term hunger and leads to organ damage, weakened immunity, and eventually death. It affects people of all ages but hits children and the elderly the hardest.

Main Causes of Starvation

  • Poverty and lack of access to food
  • War and conflict disrupting food supply
  • Climate change affecting crops and farming
  • Economic inequality and poor infrastructure

Why It Matters

  • Over 828 million people faced hunger in 2023.
  • 45 million children under age 5 suffer from wasting (low weight for height).
  • Every year, 3.1 million children die due to poor nutrition.
  • Hunger affects learning, development, and entire communities.
