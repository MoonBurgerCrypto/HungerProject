Important Facts About Starvation

  • Approximately 9 million people die of hunger and hunger-related diseases every year.
  • Children are the most vulnerable — nearly 45 million suffer from wasting.
  • One in nine people around the world go to bed hungry every night.
  • Food insecurity is worsened by conflict, climate change, and poverty.
  • Starvation can lead to irreversible physical and mental damage in children.
  • Access to clean water, healthcare, and education can help break the hunger cycle.
  • 1 in 3 people globally don’t have access to enough nutritious food.
  • Food waste is a major issue — over 1.3 billion tons of food is wasted annually.
  • Investing in small-scale farmers can greatly reduce hunger.
  • Organizations like UNICEF and WFP work daily to combat global hunger.

Why Knowing These Facts Matters

Understanding the scale of global hunger helps us recognize the urgency of the issue. By spreading awareness, supporting solutions, and taking action, we can make a difference.
